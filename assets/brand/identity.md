# Finplaza Brand Identity & Voice Guide

> **@finplaza** · Indian personal finance, made loud and clear.

---

## 1. Brand Promise

**Money moves explained loud, clear, and without the jargon — so you act smart, not scared.**

---

## 2. Personality in 5 Adjectives

1. **Bold** — we say it like it is, no fluff.
2. **Energetic** — fast pace, high contrast, scroll-stopping.
3. **Clear** — complex finance broken into plain Hindi-English.
4. **Trustworthy** — facts first, hype never.
5. **Youthful** — built for the 20s-30s phone-first viewer.

---

## 3. Brand Voice — Do / Don't

We sound **confident and high-energy**, but we are **educators, not advisors**.

| ✅ DO | ❌ DON'T |
|------|---------|
| "Here's how this credit card's reward structure actually works." | "Buy this card and get rich." |
| "The RBI cut rates yesterday — here's what it means for your EMI." | Predict where rates/markets go next. |
| "Big number alert: this savings account gives X% — let's break it down." | "Guaranteed returns" / "multibagger" / "can't-lose deal." |
| Use punchy, short, active sentences. | Use fear-mongering or clickbait that the video can't deliver. |
| Explain trade-offs honestly (fees, lock-ins, eligibility). | Tell anyone to **buy, sell, or hold** anything. |
| Say "This is education, not financial advice." | Imply we know your personal situation. |

**Golden rule:** Energy goes into the *delivery*, never into the *promise*. Hype the explanation, never the outcome.

## 4. Using the Fonts

**Display — `font-family: "Anton, Impact, sans-serif"`**
- Heavy, condensed, attention-grabbing.
- **ALWAYS UPPERCASE.**
- Use for: thumbnail headlines, big on-screen numbers, section titles, the 1-3 power words that stop the scroll.
- Keep it short — Anton is for **3-5 words max** per line. It loses punch in paragraphs.

**Body — `font-family: "Inter, system-ui, sans-serif"`**
- Clean, readable, calm.
- Use **Sentence case** (not uppercase).
- Use for: sub-headlines, captions, lower-thirds, disclaimers, descriptions, any sentence longer than a few words.

**Quick rule:** If it should be *shouted*, it's Anton in CAPS. If it should be *read*, it's Inter in sentence case.

## 5. Colour Usage Rules

| Role | Hex | Use it for |
|------|-----|-----------|
| **Background** | `#0B1220` (deep navy) | Default canvas for everything. |
| **Background Alt** | `#111A2E` | Cards, panels, sections that sit on the navy. |
| **Accent — Money Green** | `#16C784` | The **brand colour**. Logo, highlights, positives, "good news," CTAs. |
| **Accent Soft** | `#1FE08F` | Green gradients, hovers, glows on the money green. |
| **Warn — Gold** | `#F6C544` | **Big numbers only** — rates, ₹ amounts, percentages. Makes figures pop. |
| **Text** | `#F4F7FB` (off-white) | Default text on navy. |
| **Text Muted** | `#9AA7BD` | Captions, secondary info, disclaimers. |
| **Danger** | `#FF5C5C` | **Alerts & compliance ONLY** — warnings, "watch out," disclaimers, deadlines. Never decorative. |

**Core logic:**
- **Navy** = the stage. **Green** = us and the good stuff. **Gold** = the numbers that matter. **Red** = stop-and-read warnings only.
- Never use red just because it looks bold. Red means *caution*, always.
- One accent leads per frame — don't fight green and gold for attention; gold belongs to the figure, green to everything else.

## 6. Thumbnail Text Formula

Stopping the scroll is a system, not luck. Every thumbnail follows the **TENSION + NUMBER + PAYOFF** pattern:

- **TENSION** — a question or stakes ("LOSING MONEY?")
- **NUMBER** — one gold figure that earns the click ("18%", "₹50,000")
- **PAYOFF** — what they'll learn ("HERE'S THE FIX")

Keep it to **3-5 Anton words in CAPS**, one gold number, max two colours of text.

## 7. The One Rule Above All

> **Everything we make stays inside the SEBI-safe lane: education and post-facto news only — never buy/sell/hold, target prices, "multibagger," or return predictions; crypto carries the ASCI disclaimer; and no forex-trading promotion. Ever.**
